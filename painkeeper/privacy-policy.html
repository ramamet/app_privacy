PainKeeper Privacy Policy

A private body map for your pain

Last updated: July 2026

🔒 Our Commitment

PainKeeper is built privacy-first. Your health is personal, and the pain you record about it belongs to you and only you.

PainKeeper has no account and no servers. Everything you track stays on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal or health data.

📊 Data Collection

We want to be completely transparent: PainKeeper does not collect any personal or health data.

  • No sign-up, no account, no profile
  • No personal information is collected
  • No usage analytics or tracking
  • No advertising and no advertising identifiers
  • No third-party SDKs that collect your data

💾 Data Storage

Everything you create in PainKeeper is stored locally on your device, in app-private storage that other apps cannot read:

  • Pain entries: The body area, side, intensity, pain type, duration, and the date and time you record, stored in a private on-device database
  • Triggers, medication & relief: Any triggers, medication, or relief you note, and whether something helped, stored on device
  • Notes: Any free-text notes you add to an entry, stored on device
  • Settings: Your reminder time, theme, and preferences, stored on device

None of this is ever uploaded to a cloud server or shared with us or any third party.

📱 Permissions

PainKeeper requests only the minimum permissions needed, and the core tracker works without any of them:

Permission Purpose Type
Notifications Optional local reminders to check in, and the “did it help?” follow-up. No content leaves your device. Optional
Run at startup Re-schedules your local reminder after the phone restarts. Works entirely on-device. Optional
Vibration Subtle haptic feedback when you tap and confirm actions. Optional
Internet Used only by Google Play to process the optional one-time Pro purchase. No personal data is sent. Billing only

PainKeeper does not use your camera or photos, does not access your location or contacts, and never records audio. All pain tracking works fully offline.

🩺 Not Medical Advice

PainKeeper is a personal tracking and note-keeping tool to help you stay organized and remember what you experienced. It is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Any trends, patterns, or reports PainKeeper shows are simply summaries of what you entered. They are not clinical findings. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional about your symptoms and before making any health decisions. The doctor report is a convenience summary you can choose to share with your clinician; it is not a substitute for professional care.

💳 Purchases

PainKeeper is free. It offers one optional one-time in-app purchase, “PainKeeper Pro,” which unlocks extra features. There is no subscription and there are no ads.

Payments are processed entirely by Google Play Billing. We never see, receive, or store your payment details. Your Pro status is remembered locally on your device and, through Google Play, tied to your Google account so you can restore it after reinstalling.

📦 Backup & Restore

PainKeeper can export your data to a single backup file so you can move to a new phone or keep a copy. This is completely under your control:

  • The backup file is created on your device. It is only ever shared if you choose to share or save it somewhere.
  • Restoring reads a backup file that you pick.
  • Backups are never sent to us or to any cloud automatically. PainKeeper has no servers to send them to.

📄 Reports & Exports

PainKeeper can generate a doctor report as a PDF or CSV file from your own entries. The file is created on your device and is only ever shared if you choose to send or save it. We never receive a copy.

🗑️ Data Deletion

You have complete control over your data:

  • In the app: Delete individual pain entries at any time.
  • Complete removal: Uninstalling PainKeeper permanently deletes all of its data from your device.

Because nothing you record ever leaves your device, there is no data held on our servers to request deletion of.

👶 Children's Privacy

PainKeeper is not directed at children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect any information from children. Since the app collects no data from any user, this policy applies equally to users of all ages.

🔄 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. Since PainKeeper collects no personal data, changes would only reflect updates to app functionality or permissions.
